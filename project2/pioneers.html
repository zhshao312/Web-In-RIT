Doug Englebart

An American engineer and inventor, and an early computer and Internet pioneer. He is best Known for his work discovered the area of human-computer interaction. Especially the invention of the computer mouse, and the development of hypertext, networked computers. Also, he is the pioneer of graphical user interfaces.

Ted Nelson

An American pioneer of information technology, philosopher, and sociologist. He created the terms hypertext and hypermedia in 1963, then he published them in 1965. Aslo, he coined the terms of transclusion, virtuality and interwingularity.

Steve Jobs

An American information technology entrepreneur and inventer. He was the chairman and officer of Apple Inc. Jobs is widely recognized as a pioneer of the microcomputer revolution of the 1970s. Jobs's official biographer, Walter Isaacson, described him as a "creative entrepreneur whose passion for perfection and ferocious drive revolutionized six industries: personal computers, animated movies, music, phones, tablet computing, and digital publishing."

Tim Berners-Lee

An English computer scientist. He is best known as the inventor of the World Wide Web. He made a proposal for an information system in 1989, and he implemented the first successful communication between a Hypertext Transfer Protocol(HTTP) client and server on the Internet.

Marc Andreessen

An American entrepreneur, invester, and software engineer. He is the first widely used Web browser and cofounder of Netscape. Andreessen is also a cofounder of Ning which is a company provides a platform for social networking websites.

Rob Hartill

A computer programmer and web designer. He is best known for his work on the Internet Movie Database website and Apache web server. Also, he played an important role in original growth of the World Wide Web.

Lou Montulli

A computer programmer. He is well known for his work in producing web browser. He co-authored a text web browser called Lynx in 1991 and 1992. This browser was one the first available and is still in use today.
